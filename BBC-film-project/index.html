About The Greatest Film List

The 100 Greatest Films of the 21st Century is a list compiled in August 2016 by the British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC) based on a voting poll of 177 film critics from around the world.

Out of the 177 film critics surveyed, 100 were either from the United States or the United Kingdom. The list is said to be heavily biased toward Western films, with no South Asian films represented.

How do Western critics select their greatest films? What notable films might have been overlooked due to this bias?

While it's challenging to compare the list to the entirety of film history, these results provide insight into the preferences of critics from various countries and the films they prioritize.

How US Critics Vote Differently From the Rest of the World

Hollywood and American films have exerted a significant influence on the global cinematic landscape. Do US critics exhibit a preference for films from their own country and culture over those from other regions?

Examining the voting patterns of US critics compared to non-US critics, a clear trend of favoritism towards US films among US critics emerges.
